🩺 Definition

Inflammation is the body's reaction against any external stimulus including microbes, viruses, physical factors such as electricity and burns, and chemical factors such as acids and alkalis.

The most common inflammation in surgery is Bacterial Inflammation.

📚 Types of Bacterial Inflammation

  • Acute Inflammation
  • Chronic Inflammation
  • Specific Inflammation (Tuberculosis)
  • Non-Specific Inflammation

Acute Inflammation

  • Boils
  • Abscess
  • Carbuncle
  • Cellulitis
  • Inflammation of wounds after operations
  • Tetanus
  • Tuberculous lymphadenitis
  • Gas Gangrene

🦠 Non-Specific Inflammations

1- Abscess

Collection of pus under the skin or inside an internal organ such as the liver.

2- Carbuncle

Group of boils usually affecting diabetic patients, commonly on the back or neck.

3- Cellulitis

Inflammation of tissues beneath the skin that does not form an abscess. It may spread to the blood causing bacteremia.

4- Post-operative Wound Infection

Usually appears on the 3rd to 5th day with redness, pain and pus discharge.

🤒 Symptoms

Local Symptoms

  • Pain
  • Swelling
  • Redness
  • Warmth
  • Loss of movement
  • Loss of function

General Symptoms

  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Sweating
  • Lethargy
  • Increased pulse

💊 Treatment

  1. Complete rest.
  2. Elevate the inflamed limb.
  3. Give enough fluids.
  4. Administer antibiotics and analgesics.
  5. Apply warm compresses.
  6. Drain pus in abscesses and carbuncles.

Important Note:
Do NOT open cellulitis because it may lead to septicemia.

🧫 Specific Inflammations

Erysipelas

Skin infection caused by Streptococci. It is contagious and the patient should be isolated.

Lymphadenitis

Inflammation and enlargement of lymph nodes in the neck, axilla or groin.

Tetanus

Anaerobic bacterial infection affecting nerves causing jaw stiffness, difficulty in breathing and generalized convulsions.

Gas Gangrene

Anaerobic infection producing gas, foul smell and muscle necrosis. Requires urgent treatment and isolation.

🩹 Wounds

A wound is a cut in the skin and underlying tissues caused by injury, burns, or surgery.

Types of Wounds

  • Closed Wounds
  • Open Wounds
  • Compound Wounds
  • Surgical Wounds

Factors Delaying Healing

  • Necrotic tissue
  • Poor nutrition
  • Vitamin C deficiency
  • Microbial infection
  • Poor blood circulation

🔥 Burns

Causes

  • Heat
  • Electricity
  • Radiation
  • Acids & Chemicals

Degrees

  1. First Degree
  2. Second Degree
  3. Third Degree
  4. Fourth Degree
  5. Fifth Degree
  6. Sixth Degree

🦠 Surgical Infections

Surgical infection is an infection requiring surgical intervention or occurring after surgery.

Complications

  • Tissue destruction
  • Abscess
  • Lymphadenitis
  • Pleural & Pericardial infection
  • Fistula
  • Secondary infection

Treatment

  • Incision & Drainage
  • Excision
  • Appropriate Antibiotics

💉 Cellulitis

Cellulitis is a non-suppurative inflammation affecting connective tissues and is commonly caused by Streptococci.

  • Rest
  • Warm Compresses
  • Elevate the Limb
  • Penicillin

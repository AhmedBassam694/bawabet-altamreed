🩺 Definition

Inflammation is the body's reaction against any external stimulus including microbes, viruses, physical factors such as electricity and burns, and chemical factors such as acids and alkalis.

The most common inflammation in surgery is Bacterial Inflammation.

📌 Important Note:
The most common inflammation in surgery is Bacterial Inflammation.

📚 Types of Bacterial Inflammation

  • Acute Inflammation
  • Chronic Inflammation
  • Specific Inflammation (Tuberculosis)
  • Non-Specific Inflammation

Acute Inflammation

  • Boils
  • Abscess
  • Carbuncle
  • Cellulitis
  • Inflammation of wounds after operations
  • Tetanus
  • Tuberculous Lymphadenitis
  • Gas Gangrene
💡 Exam Tip:
Tuberculosis = Specific Inflammation

🦠 Non-Specific Inflammations

1- Abscess

Collection of pus under the skin or inside an internal organ such as the liver.

2- Carbuncle

A group of boils usually affecting diabetic patients, especially on the back or neck.

3- Cellulitis

Inflammation of tissues beneath the skin that does not form an abscess. Its danger is the spread of microbes into the blood causing bacteremia.

4- Post-operative Wound Infection

Usually appears on the 3rd to 5th day with redness, pain and pus discharge.

🤒 Symptoms

Local Symptoms

  • Pain
  • Swelling
  • Redness
  • Warmth
  • Loss of movement
  • Loss of function

General Symptoms

  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Sweating
  • Lethargy
  • Increased pulse

💊 Treatment

  1. Complete rest.
  2. Elevate the inflamed limb.
  3. Give enough fluids.
  4. Administer antibiotics and analgesics.
  5. Apply warm compresses.
  6. Drain pus in abscesses and carbuncles.
⚠️ Warning:
Never open cellulitis because it may lead to septicemia.

🧫 Specific Inflammations

1- Erysipelas

Skin infection caused by Streptococci. It is contagious and the patient should be isolated.

2- Lymphadenitis

Inflammation and enlargement of lymph nodes in the neck, axilla or groin.

3- Tetanus

Anaerobic bacterial infection causing jaw stiffness, difficulty in breathing and generalized convulsions.

4- Gas Gangrene

Anaerobic infection producing gas, foul smell and muscle necrosis. Requires urgent treatment and isolation.

🩹 Wounds

A wound is a cut in the skin and underlying tissues caused by injury, burns or surgical procedures.

Types of Wounds

  • Closed Wounds
  • Open Wounds
  • Compound Wounds
  • Surgical Wounds

Common Symptoms

  • Pain
  • Bleeding
  • Loss of Function
💡 Exam Tip:
Clean surgical wounds should remain closed, while contaminated wounds require regular dressing.

🔥 Burns

Causes

  • Heat
  • Electricity
  • Radiation
  • Chemicals (Acids & Alkalis)

Degrees of Burns

  1. First Degree
  2. Second Degree
  3. Third Degree
  4. Fourth Degree
  5. Fifth Degree
  6. Sixth Degree
📌 Minor burns affect less than 15% of the body surface.

🦠 Surgical Infections

Surgical infections are infections that require surgical intervention or occur after surgery.

Complications

  • Abscess
  • Tissue destruction
  • Lymphadenitis
  • Fistula
  • Secondary infection

Treatment

  • Incision and Drainage
  • Excision
  • Antibiotics

💉 Cellulitis

Cellulitis is a non-suppurative inflammation of connective tissue caused mainly by Streptococci.

  • Rest
  • Warm Compresses
  • Elevation of the affected limb
  • Penicillin
⚠️ If there is no improvement within 24 hours, suspect an abscess or resistant bacteria.
